01 · Current State

Where Things Stand Today

Three platforms, three kinds of headroom. All of it good news.

I went through Toot 'n Totum's channels the way a new follower would, and the takeaway is genuinely exciting. The brand equity is enormous, the visual identity is sharp, and the biggest wins are still sitting on the table waiting to be picked up.

TikTok

The open lane

TikTok rewards exactly what Toot 'n Totum has in abundance: real places, real people, and daily rituals worth filming. Right now this is the brand's most untapped audience, which means there is no baggage and no bad habits to undo. The lane is wide open for a presence that feels native from day one.

The opportunity: become the first Amarillo brand people follow for fun, not just for deals.

Instagram

The warm-up act

The account shows up consistently and keeps the brand looking polished, which is a real foundation. The opportunity is to point the camera outward: more faces, more stores, more video. A promo graphic earns a glance. A Reel about the cashier who knows everyone's order earns a share.

The opportunity: shift from announcements to moments, and let Reels carry the reach.

Facebook

The home crowd

This is where Toot 'n Totum's most loyal guests already gather, and practical updates land well here. The opportunity is conversation. Fast replies, shout-outs by name, and posts that ask Amarillo a question can turn a scrolling audience into a commenting one.

The opportunity: treat every comment like a guest walking through the door.

02 · The Core Recommendation

Stop broadcasting.
Start connecting.

Toot 'n Totum does not need social media to introduce itself to Amarillo. The city already fuels up here, grabs lunch here, washes the truck here, and supports its schools through programs like Spirit Pump. The brand is woven into daily life in a way most companies spend millions trying to imitate.

So the job of the feed is not to broadcast at that community. It is to reflect it back. Less megaphone, more neighbor. When the channels sound the way the stores feel, engagement stops being a metric to chase and becomes a habit guests keep.

70+ locations Hundreds of team members Thousands of daily guests

That is not an audience to reach. That is a community to feature.

Rooted in Toot 'n Totum's Values

This philosophy is really just the company's own values translated to the feed. Guest Ready means answering every comment like someone just walked up to the counter. Honor and Value means celebrating team members and guests by name and treating their stories with care. Quick Clean and Convenient means content that respects a thirty-second attention span and always looks sharp. Ownership means every post gets measured, learned from, and made better the next week. And Team-Focused means the people behind the counters are the stars, never just the logo.

03 · Platform Strategy

One Voice, Three Jobs

The same warm personality everywhere, with each platform doing what it does best.

TikTok

Discovery: where the Panhandle finds its next favorite stop
  • Build recurring series instead of one-offs: store tours, fountain drink combos, satisfying car wash runs, and a day in the life of a shift.
  • Treat TikTok like a search engine. Captions and on-screen text should say Amarillo, breakfast, car wash, and road trip, because that is how people search now.
  • Let team members host. A familiar face in a familiar store will always beat a polished ad.
  • Jump on local moments: game days, school spirit weeks, and the famous Panhandle weather.
Cadence: 3 to 4 posts per week, filmed in batches during store visits

Instagram

Connection: where the brand's personality lives
  • Lead with Reels and let short-form video do the heavy lifting on reach.
  • Turn the grid into a community album: employee spotlights, guest features, and local partnerships.
  • Use Stories daily for the playful stuff: polls, this-or-that votes, and new item sneak peeks.
  • Repost guest photos and videos with permission, so followers see themselves in the feed.
Cadence: 4 to 5 Reels per week, Stories daily, grid posts that earn their spot

Facebook

Community: where loyal guests already gather
  • Keep the practical wins coming: fuel deals, REWARDS updates, and new store news belong here.
  • Add a conversation layer with questions, polls, and shout-outs that invite comments.
  • Answer every comment, message, and review within one business day.
  • Give philanthropy and school partnerships a bigger stage. The hometown crowd celebrates loudest here.
Cadence: 4 to 5 posts per week with daily community management
04 · The Community Content Model

People First, Always

Why employee and community-driven content is the highest leverage move on the board.

Most brands have to manufacture a community before they can market to one. Toot 'n Totum already has the real thing: more than 70 locations, hundreds of team members, and guests who stop by every single day, sometimes twice. That kind of local density is a content engine no agency can replicate, and it is sitting there ready to be switched on.

People follow people. A logo can inform, but a familiar face invites. When the feed features the cashier who remembers your order, the store that fuels your Friday nights, and the guests who make each location feel like theirs, every post becomes something the right audience wants to share: the neighbors.

1

Capture

Give stores a simple way to send in raw moments: a monthly theme calendar, a quick consent step, and a phone. No production burden on busy teams.

2

Feature

The coordinator shapes those moments into spotlight posts, recurring series, and Reels with one consistent, warm voice across every platform.

3

Amplify

Featured team members, schools, and guests share the posts to their own circles, and the reach compounds without a dollar of ad spend.

Bonus: every employee spotlight quietly doubles as the best recruiting ad Toot 'n Totum could run.
